## Summary

The article provides a comprehensive overview of sharing and deploying agents in Copilot Studio, emphasizing the complexities surrounding permissions, roles, and governance. It clarifies the requirements for collaboration, the differences in deployment methods, and the implications of permissions across various channels, particularly in Teams and SharePoint.

## Key Points

- Users must have access to Copilot Studio, be designated as Editors, and hold the Environment Maker role to collaborate on agents.
- Viewer permissions do not apply universally; in SharePoint, access is governed by site permissions rather than Copilot Studio settings.
- Two distinct deployment methods for Teams exist: a low-friction shared link method and a more controlled admin-approved app method.
